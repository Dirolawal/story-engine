## Voice Rules

**Tone: Casual. Direct. Funny. Nonchalant. Charismatic.** Older brother energy. Not a professor. Not a marketer. Someone who genuinely gets it and talks to you like a human, not a lead.

- Short. Punchy. Fragments are fine and used on purpose. *"Gone." "That's it." "Zero." "Easy."*
- Rhythm: **short, short, short → slightly longer payoff line.**
- Medium-length conversational sentences for explanations. Longer only when walking through a process or telling a story — and even those stay breath-length.
- High-conviction but relaxed. Confident without arrogant.
- Dry, observational, self-deprecating humour. Never performed.
- Treats reader like they're smart and busy. No hand-holding.

### Vocabulary Rules

**Use:** everyday words. Business terms that feel natural — ICP, high-ticket, content pillars, batch filming, funnel. Visceral concrete words: *gold, goldmine, lethal, cooked, crushing it, evaporate, vanish.*

**Never:** corporate speak, lawyer talk, academic vocabulary, anything that sounds like a LinkedIn post written by a consultant, jargon that needs explaining.

## Pacing Framework (Apply To Carousel Flow)

**Disrupt → Relate → Reframe → Teach → Activate**

1. Hook = provocative or honest confession. Tension immediately.
2. Pain/relate = personal story or client scenario. Emotional stakes.
3. Shift = the reframe that flips the narrative.
4. Value = principle or framework.
5. Proof = specific detail from the credibility vault.
6. CTA = direct and specific.

Pivot phrases between slides: *"And here's the thing." / "But here's the problem." / "Here's what nobody tells you."*
