## Hook Scoring Framework (Score Slide 1)

The hook slide is the only thing that matters for stopping the scroll. Before finalising the hook, score it against these 9 questions. The more boxes it checks, the harder it stops the scroll.

1. **TANGIBLE** — concrete? Specific objects, names, numbers, scenes?
2. **SUPER DESIRABLE** — payoff is something the audience badly wants?
3. **SIMPLE** — understood in 2 seconds? Zero jargon?
4. **CONTROVERSIAL** — challenges a held belief? Pattern interrupt?
5. **RELATABLE** — uses the audience's actual language?
6. **EASY** — implied solution feels achievable? "One tiny shift" not "make 10k in 5 minutes"?
7. **ANTICIPATION** — curiosity gap that demands closing?
8. **PRESSING** — a question the audience is already asking at 1am?
9. **IDENTIFY** — viewer sees themselves in the first 2 seconds?

**Minimum viable:** SIMPLE plus ANTICIPATION (or PRESSING). Without those two, nothing else matters.

**Killer stack:** TANGIBLE plus RELATABLE plus IDENTIFY plus CONTROVERSIAL plus ANTICIPATION = breakout potential.

**Reject any hook scoring below 4/9.** Rewrite the hook before returning.

When generating hook variations or carousel openers, include a brief score comment in the hook slide's direction field. Format: `Hook score 7/9: Tangible, Simple, Relatable, Easy, Anticipation, Pressing, Identify. Missing: Controversial, Desirable.`

## Pacing Framework (Apply To Carousel Flow)

**Disrupt, Relate, Reframe, Teach, Activate.**

1. Hook = provocative or honest confession. Tension immediately.
2. Pain/relate = personal story or client scenario. Emotional stakes.
3. Shift = the reframe that flips the narrative.
4. Value = principle or framework.
5. Proof = specific detail from the credibility vault below or the story bible.
6. CTA = direct and specific.

Pivot phrases between slides: *"And here's the thing." / "But here's the problem." / "Here's what nobody tells you."*
