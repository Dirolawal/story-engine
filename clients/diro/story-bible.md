## Chapter beats

### Chapter 1 — Limerick (birth → 18)

- Born and raised in Limerick, Ireland. Nigerian-British.
- Rough all-boys primary school. Constant fighting.
- Strict parents. Heavy church involvement (6 days a week).
- Routine: school, church, training, home.
- Athletics from young age. Represented Ireland internationally.
- From age 4: "I'll be an engineer." Said it constantly. Only path shown.
- Real underlying dream: freedom. Make own decisions, travel, no ceiling.

### Chapter 2 — The leap (April 2022)

- Applied to UC New Zealand. Didn't get in.
- Backup option was staying in Limerick. Refused.
- Applied to Aberdeen on a Friday.
- Accepted Saturday.
- Moved out the same Saturday. Said bye to everyone.
- Risky, but knew freedom was the real goal.

### Chapter 3 — Uni that wasn't uni (2022 → April 2024)

- First two years were Covid era.
- Whole accommodation block did exam papers together at the kitchen table.
- Group cheating became normal.
- Year 3 hit: in-person exams, in-person learning. Couldn't cope.
- Took a gap year (a relationship situation — never for content).
- Tried jobs: B&M Retail night shifts (10pm–7am) at £7/hour. Costco selling alarms commission-only. Quit fast.
- Tried copywriting, e-com, Amazon FBA, affiliate marketing.
- Discovered content skills through affiliate marketing — first edits, first taste of virality.

### Chapter 4 — Crypto and the dropout (2022 → April 2024)

- Joined an online mentorship in 2022 (omit the brand from public content).
- Studied crypto deeply through 2023.
- Saved £7k over a year. Put it all in summer 2023.
- October 2023: down to £2k.
- November 2023: flipped £2k to £30k. 15x.
- Sat in a lecture making £2k in a day. Realised the gap.
- Started travelling: Dubai, Spain, Ayia Napa, Berlin x3, Thailand. World opened up.
- Walked out of December 2023 exams with a blank paper.
- Officially dropped out April 2024.
- **Lied to his parents about being in uni for nearly a year.** ("I failed" → "resets in summer" → "resets in winter" → silence → eventually told them.)

### Chapter 5 — The camera (January 2024 onwards)

- Bought first camera January 2024 to start a personal-brand YouTube channel.
- Recorded 3 videos. Posted zero. Fear.
- Started shooting friends as practice. Free or near-free.
- First paid shoot: £15.
- Moved into a 3-bed house in Aberdeen.
- Picked up freelance: nightclub events, university balls, prom shoots.
- February 2025: ~£1k in a month from gigs. Felt like part-time wages.

### Chapter 6 — Locked in (May 2024 onwards)

- Best friend (an artist and musician) moves into the Aberdeen flat.
- Same brain, same mission: don't grind for a salary forever.
- Turned the flat into a studio.
- Music videos, visualisers, performance videos, effects shoots.
- Practice multiplier: build, shoot, edit, repeat. Every week.
- **The dad full-circle:** at 12-14, Diro was his dad's backup cameraman at church events across Ireland. Dad gave it up when Diro went to school. Diro picked it up when he dropped out. Generational baton.

### Chapter 7 — Rock bottom (March 2025 → January 2026)

- March 2025: shattered patella tendon. Surgery.
- Bed-bound 4 months. Couldn't sit up, couldn't shoot, couldn't earn.
- Questioned if he'd walk properly again.
- Mentally lowest point.
- Used the time to study editing on YouTube (silver lining).
- Off the crutches by July.
- August 2025: travel reignited him (Malta, Portugal, Berlin).
- August 2025: lost £28k in crypto in a single day.
- October 2025: Apple job. £800/month.
- Paid a £2.5k creative direction mentor in instalments. Effectively unpaid 3 of 4 Apple months.
- December 2025: end-of-contract interviews. Didn't get extended.
- January 2026: contract ended. Officially unemployed and broke.

### Chapter 8 — The February decision (February 2026)

- Conversation with a friend about uncertainty:
  > "I don't know how I'll pay rent next month. But I wouldn't have it any other way."
- Considered moving back to parents' house.
- Old athletics friend from Ireland (only ever saw each other at Munsters / All-Irelands / Internationals as kids) liked one of his videos.
- Reached out. Hopped on a call.
- The friend was in Marbella, running ads, building a high-ticket sales business.
- Offered: 5 days videography in Marbella for £450, before flying to Miami.

### Chapter 9 — The cascade (March 2026)

- Agreed at 4pm. Used £300 of the £450 to buy a new camera that night.
- Packed in 2 hours. Said bye to the flatmate. Left.
- Marbella: slept on the couch. Recorded everything.
- Content slapped. The team loved it.
- The owner of the offer (in America) flew Diro to Miami — full £900 flights paid + accommodation.
- Miami: closed 4 high-ticket guys at £600 each = £2,400 in a week. Triple Apple wage.
- After Miami: Cape Town for what was supposed to be 3 weeks, became 5+.
- Met entrepreneurs doing 20k, 30k, 100k months. Went to a mastermind.
- Identity shifted. Closed more deals. Invested £5k into a creative-direction mentor.
- Pivoted offer: from videographer-only to content systems / creative direction.
- Left Cape Town with 2 high-ticket clients on retainer.

### Chapter 10 — Now (April–May 2026)

- Spent more of 2026 abroad than home: Spain, Portugal, Marbella, Miami, Cape Town.
- London soon, then Bali.
- Just closed biggest retainer ever — 3 months + flights paid to Bali.
- Bali for 3 months: mastermind + work + network.
- Helping coaches and high-ticket experts turn content from a stress into their biggest asset.
- Premium content systems for people who already know what they're doing — they just need someone to capture it.

## Voice anchors for "my story" content

**Sentence rhythm.** Short. Short. Build. Land.
**Specifics over abstracts.** Numbers, places, dates, tools. Always.
**Vulnerability before credibility.** Then credibility lands harder.
**Stranger frame.** The reader knows nothing about you. Treat every story as a first impression.

**Phrases that fit text-on-image format:**
"Here's the thing." / "At the end of the day." / "Imagine..." / "It's not hard."

**Phrases that don't fit slide text (save for video and conversation):**
"Do you get me?" / "Bro." / "100%." / "Exactly."
